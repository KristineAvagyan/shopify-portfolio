Discover Armenia

A Land of Eternal Beauty

Nestled in the heart of the Caucasus, Armenia is one of the world's oldest civilizations — a tapestry of faith, resilience, and breathtaking landscapes.

✦

Ancient Heritage

Over 3,000 years of continuous civilization. The first nation to adopt Christianity in 301 AD, Armenia stands as a beacon of faith and cultural preservation.

Mountain Majesty

A highland nation where 90% of the territory sits above 1,000 meters. From the biblical Mount Ararat to the peaks of Aragats, every horizon tells a story.

❖

Living Culture

A unique alphabet, ancient music, sacred dances, and culinary traditions passed down through millennia — Armenia's culture breathes in every corner.

Through the Ages

A Journey Through Time

From the dawn of civilization to modern independence, Armenia's history is etched in stone, scripture, and song.

2400 BC

Kingdom of Urartu

The ancient kingdom flourishes around Lake Van, building fortresses and irrigation systems that still awe archaeologists today.

301 AD

First Christian Nation

Armenia becomes the first country to adopt Christianity as its state religion, forever shaping its identity, art, and architecture.

405 AD

The Armenian Alphabet

Mesrop Mashtots creates the unique Armenian script, preserving the nation's language and literature through centuries of adversity.

1915

The Armenian Genocide

A dark chapter of immense suffering — 1.5 million Armenians perished. Their memory lives eternal in the hearts of the diaspora.

1991

Independence Restored

Following the collapse of the Soviet Union, Armenia reclaims its sovereignty and begins a new chapter as a modern democratic nation.

Untamed Beauty

Nature's Masterpiece

From alpine lakes to ancient forests, Armenia's landscapes are a symphony of color, light, and silence.

Alpine Lake

Lake Sevan

The "Pearl of Armenia" — one of the largest freshwater high-altitude lakes in the world, shimmering at 1,900 meters above sea level.

Ancient Forest

Dilijan National Park

Armenia's "Little Switzerland" — misty forests, mineral springs, and medieval monasteries hidden among the trees.

Sacred Mountain

Mount Ararat

The biblical resting place of Noah's Ark — a majestic 5,137m peak that dominates the Armenian horizon.

Red Canyon

Noravank Gorge

Dramatic orange-red cliffs frame this 13th-century monastery, creating one of Armenia's most iconic vistas.

National Symbol

The Pomegranate

Symbol of fertility, abundance, and life — the pomegranate is woven into Armenian art, cuisine, and identity.

Tatev Monastery

Perched on a cliff edge, accessible by the world's longest reversible cable car.

13th Century

Noravank

A masterpiece nestled in a dramatic red-orange canyon gorge.

The Cradle of Wine

Armenia is home to the world's oldest known winery — 6,100 years old, discovered in the Areni-1 cave. Today, Armenian winemakers blend ancient karas (clay vessel) traditions with modern excellence, producing wines of extraordinary depth and character.

Questions

Frequently Asked

When is the best time to visit Armenia?
+
Late spring (May-June) and early autumn (September-October) offer perfect weather, blooming landscapes, and comfortable temperatures for exploring both cities and mountains.
Is Armenia safe for tourists?
+
Yes, Armenia is one of the safest countries in the region. Crime rates are low, and Armenians are known for their exceptional hospitality toward visitors.
What language is spoken in Armenia?
+
Armenian is the official language. Russian is widely understood, and English is increasingly spoken, especially in Yerevan and tourist areas.
Do I need a visa to visit Armenia?
+
Citizens of many countries can enter Armenia visa-free for up to 180 days. Check the official government website for your specific nationality.
What currency is used?
+
The Armenian Dram (AMD). Credit cards are widely accepted in cities, but carry cash for rural areas and small vendors.
